This service provides a platform for documenting and sharing data. KTH's employees, students, and external partners can collaborate and manage access to different types of data at varying levels of access. All systems and services provided by KTH must be used in accordance with current regulations, including guidelines, rules, and current legislation. Failure to comply with these regulations may result in a user being banned from the system. All users of this service must also agree to KTH's liability agreement. Users are responsible for following the general rules for the use of KTH's system, which can be found in the liability agreement link. Additionally, users are responsible for managing data in accordance with other relevant guidelines at KTH, which can be found below.

This service is intended to be used to support data management within KTH activities, which include education, research, support activities, and trade union activities. For research activities, users must follow KTH's Guidelines for Handling Research Data link.

Confidential information is information that, if publicly disclosed, poses a risk of harm to KTH or individuals. Confidential information should not be made publicly available but may be shared within a restricted community if a risk assessment has concluded that this is in line with acceptable risk. See instructions for information security at KTH link.

For example, users should not make personal data or someone else's copyrighted material publicly available without obtaining permission or consent. For more information, see KTH's guideline for intellectual assets link, as well as guidelines for publicity and confidentiality link and information on personal data processing link.

If you are uncertain whether material collected as raw data in a research project is confidential material or what access level is appropriate, contact researchdata@kth.se for advice.

Processing of personal data:

When you create an account for this service, KTH processes your personal data in accordance with Swedish legislation and the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the EU. To close your account, contact KTH at dataskyddsombud@kth.se, and you will then be removed from the user register. If you have entered your name and optionally an ORCID when making a dataset available, the name and optional ID will remain in the system, as publishing information of your name corresponds to the author declaration on publication and where KTH, as the responsible publisher, cannot withdraw that published information.

KTH is the data controller for personal data included in data processed in KTH's research activities, but personal data processing may be regulated in an agreement with an external party. If an external party uses this service to submit datasets containing personal information to a restricted community, they must have an established agreement with KTH that regulates the processing of personal data. In this agreement, KTH can either act as a personal data processor or as one of the parties where joint personal data processing is regulated. Personal data collected in research should not be made publicly available unless explicit consent has been obtained from the subjects whose personal data is being collected. The handling of personal data is subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the EU and may also be subject to other data protection laws depending on the jurisdiction of the external party. Details of this is handled in the specific agreement with the external party. For further information regarding a specific agreement you should contact avtal@kth.se
