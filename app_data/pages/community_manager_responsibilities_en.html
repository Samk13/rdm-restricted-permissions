Terms of use and specific responsibility for you as a community manager

General terms

KTH.se/namn is a platform for documenting and sharing data. Here, KTH's employees, students, and external collaborators can together document, version control, and make different types of data available at different levels of access. All systems and services provided by KTH should be used in accordance with applicable regulations. This means that guidelines, rules, and laws should be followed for the data that is stored and made available via KTH.se/namn. If this is not followed and you misbehave, you may be suspended from the systems.

What is stated in the liability agreement that all KTH account holders sign also applies to the use of KTH.se/namn. As a user, you are responsible for following the general rules for using KTH's systems that are outlined in the liability agreement. Link as well as other relevant guidelines for KTH's operations.

KTH.se/namn is a support tool for KTH's operations, which include education, research, support activities, and union activities. Below is more detailed information specific to KTH.se/namn.

Responsibility - the creator of a community is responsible for the content.

The purpose of communities in namn is to facilitate data sharing in collaborations within KTH's areas of operation, as well as to document and make data available within the operations. KTH.se/namn may not be used for private purposes. Communities may only be made public after risk assessment, for example, in connection with a data management plan for research, and where the material shared within the community has been assessed as appropriate for open data sharing.

The person who creates a community and holds the role of community manager is responsible for data management within the community. The guidelines for handling data in research activities are described in more detail in KTH's Guideline for research data management. Link

your research group leader or KTH's Data Protection Officer for guidance.

As a community manager, it is your responsibility to ensure that the data within your community is shared at the appropriate access level. KTH.se/namn offers different access levels for data sharing, and you should choose the one that best suits the type of data being shared.

It is important to note that KTH reserves the right to remove any material that violates Swedish archive legislation. Additionally, any confidential information that may cause harm to KTH or individuals should not be made public on KTH.se/namn.

If you are unsure whether the data collected as raw material in research projects is confidential, please consult your research group leader or KTH's Data Protection Officer for guidance.researchdata@kth.se for advice. Often, confidential data can be shared within a project group with limited access in accordance with any contractual agreements, even if it is not appropriate for it to be fully publicly available.

For example, it is not permitted to make material publicly available that may result in:

  • Disclosure of public documents covered by secrecy under the Public Access to Information and Secrecy Act (2009:400)
  • Disclosure of classified information
  • Disclosure of personal data where a risk assessment has shown that it poses risks to the individual's privacy to spread it publicly
  • Unauthorized use and distribution of copyrighted material or related rights
  • KTH.se/name is not suitable for classified information.

Information that cannot be made publicly available can in most cases be shared with limited access within a limited group of authorized persons within a community in KTH.se/name.

In some cases, there are reasons for confidentiality, where it is the community manager who assesses who has authorization to access the data shared within a community with limited access. The community manager is responsible for adding only those who should have access to the data as members of the community with limited access.

If you, for any reason, want to increase the visibility of data that has been documented and made available within a community, you should first contact the community manager for this within the community where the data has been made available. In the second instance, you can contact researchdata@kth.se The levels available are as follows:

At the dataset level:

  • Full public visibility - anyone can search for and download the data files
  • Restricted access for downloading files - only members of the community have access. However, the description of the content is searchable for the public.
  • Restricted access for both downloading files and limited access and searchability for the descriptions of the files. The content in the dataset description is only searchable and available for download by members of the community.

At the community level:

  • Full public visibility - anyone can search for and download the data files
  • Restricted access - only members of the community have access

It is also possible to make a dataset description available without storing the data files described in connection with KTH.se/namn.

System administrators at KTH have access to data regardless of the accessibility level, but will only use this authority in accordance with the responsibility commitment for system administrators at KTH. Link Remember that KTH, as a state authority, is subject to the principle of public access to official documents. This means that material found on KTH.se/namn may constitute public documents, which must be disclosed upon request to access public documents unless otherwise provided by secrecy provisions.

Copyright

Copyright must be respected by all users. You should not upload copyrighted material where someone else holds the copyright unless you have the copyright holder's permission. In the event that a work has several authors, whose contributions cannot be distinguished as independent works, the copyright belongs jointly to the authors. KTH.se/namn specifies the CC-BY 4.0 license as the basis when you make a dataset available, which means that anyone can use the material freely as long as the copyright holder is cited. It is also possible to choose other licenses in accordance with KTH's guidelines. For more information, see KTH's guidelines for intellectual assets. Link

Processing of personal data

When you create an account on KTH.se/namn, KTH processes your personal data. You can contact KTH at xxxx to terminate your account. If you provide your name and possibly ORCID when making a dataset available, your name will remain in the system even if you terminate your account and other personal data processing, as it corresponds to author attribution in publishing and where KTH, as the responsible publisher, has the right/obligation to state this.

KTH is usually the data controller. If an external party uses KTH.se/namn to make datasets with restricted access containing personal data available, there must be an agreement between the external party and KTH where KTH can be a data processor or data controller, or one of the parties where joint processing of personal data is regulated in the agreement.
